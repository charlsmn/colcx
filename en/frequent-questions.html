Frequent Questions

Welcome to our Frequently Asked Questions section on carbon credits. Here we answer the most common questions about the registration and certification of emissions reduction projects. If you have any other questions, please do not hesitate to contact us.

A carbon credit or Colcer is a certificate that represents a reduction of one ton of carbon dioxide (CO₂) or its equivalent in other greenhouse gases.

The registration process involves verifying emission reduction projects, documenting the reductions achieved and issuing carbon credit certificates. This is how you can do it with COLCX (Link to certification program).

Renewable energy projects, energy efficiency, reforestation and forest conservation, waste management, among others. Know the Sectors in which COLCX works (Link to Methodologies by sector).

Any organization, company or individual implementing a project that reduces greenhouse gas emissions can apply for certification.

Access to carbon markets, generation of additional revenue, contribution to environmental sustainability and enhancement of corporate reputation.

The time may vary depending on the type of project and available documentation, but generally it can take between 4 to 6 months.
